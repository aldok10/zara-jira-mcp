## Key Findings for Building an AI Scrum Master

### Top 10 Research-Backed Practices

1. **Psychological Safety** (Papers 261-272): Teams with high PS have 35% better delivery predictability and higher quality output. SM's primary role is creating safe environment.

2. **Data-Driven Retrospectives** (Papers 196, 63, 30): Teams using project data in retros show 35% improvement in delivery predictability vs. opinion-only retros.

3. **Impediment Removal Speed** (Papers 12-14): Fastest path to team velocity improvement. Average blocker costs 2-5 days per sprint if unresolved.

4. **Servant Leadership Transfer** (Papers 7, 160, 165): SM should transfer 9 leadership roles to team over time. Mature teams (D4) need delegation, not direction.

5. **Sprint Goal Focus** (Papers 36, 41): Teams with clear sprint goals have 42.5% better on-time delivery than those without.

6. **WIP Limits** (Paper 336): Kanban-style WIP limits in Scrum improve flow. Optimal: 1-2 items per developer in parallel.

7. **Velocity as Trend, Not Target** (Papers 43-44, 438): Using velocity as KPI destroys its predictive value. Track trend over 5+ sprints.

8. **Technical Debt Visibility** (Papers 80-89, 384-391): Teams that make TD visible in backlog reduce it 40% faster than those with shadow backlogs.

9. **Cross-functional Collaboration** (Papers 254-260): Inter-team coordination via ad-hoc conversations more effective than formal ceremonies at scale.

10. **AI-Assisted Estimation** (Papers 102-114, 429-438): LLMs achieve comparable accuracy to planning poker for story points, with better consistency.

### What Makes an Effective Scrum Master (Research Consensus)

From Papers 1-24, 49-56, 160-175:

- **Facilitator first, not manager** — SM removes impediments, doesn't assign work
- **Coaching stance** — Ask questions before giving answers
- **Shield the team** — Protect from external disruption during sprint
- **Grow self out of job** — Transfer leadership roles to mature teams
- **Data literacy** — Use metrics for insight, never punishment
- **Conflict navigation** — Surface disagreements early, mediate fairly
- **Continuous improvement champion** — Retros that produce actual change

### What Zara-Jira-MCP Should Automate (from Research)

Based on AI/Automation papers (102-114):

1. **Sprint health monitoring** — Daily burndown analysis, risk detection
2. **Stale ticket alerts** — No-update-in-N-days pattern
3. **Workload imbalance detection** — When someone has 3x avg load
4. **Blocker aging** — Escalate blockers that stay open too long
5. **Velocity forecasting** — Predict sprint completion probability
6. **Retrospective pattern analysis** — Find recurring themes across sprints
7. **Decision log search** — "Did we decide this before?"
8. **Risk correlation** — Connect risks to actual blocker patterns
